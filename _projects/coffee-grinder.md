## Overview

This project focused on designing and modeling a functional **manual coffee grinder assembly** using real-world components and mechanical constraints.

The goal was to redesign a traditional grinder using **off-the-shelf parts (McMaster-Carr)** and validate the design through a fully constrained CAD assembly.

---

## Design Objective

- Convert rotational input from a handle into grinding motion  
- Use a **miter gear system** to transfer motion between perpendicular shafts  
- Ensure the system is physically buildable (no interference, no unsupported components)  
- Integrate both **coffee bean storage** and **ground coffee collection**

---

## Mechanical System

The grinder consists of:

- Hand crank → rotational input  
- Horizontal shaft → transfers motion  
- **Miter gears (90°)** → redirect motion vertically  
- Vertical shaft → drives grinding mechanism  
- Conical burrs → grind coffee beans  

The system ensures that when the handle rotates, the burrs rotate continuously to grind coffee.

---

## CAD & Engineering Approach

- Selected real components from **McMaster-Carr**  
- Modeled custom parts (limited to fabrication constraints)  
- Built a **fully constrained assembly in Fusion 360**  
- Ensured:
  - No part interference  
  - All components remain supported  
  - Assembly behaves like a real physical system  

---

## Key Challenges

- Aligning miter gears for proper motion transfer  
- Designing shaft connections and constraints  
- Maintaining realistic assembly (no “floating” parts)  
- Balancing manufacturability with design complexity
