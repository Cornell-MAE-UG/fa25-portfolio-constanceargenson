## Overview

This project involved analyzing the performance of a **liquid–liquid heat exchanger system**, comparing **parallel flow** and **counterflow configurations** using experimental data.

The goal was to evaluate how flow configuration impacts heat transfer efficiency and understand the system through **mass, energy, and entropy balances**.

---

## System Description

The system consists of:

- A heat exchanger connecting **hot and cold water reservoirs**  
- Continuous flow through tubing on both sides  
- A thin plate separating the fluids, allowing heat transfer via conduction  
- Pump-driven circulation between reservoirs  

As shown in the setup :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1} (page 3), the system can be configured as either:
- **Parallel flow** (fluids move in same direction)  
- **Counterflow** (fluids move in opposite directions)  

---

## Flow Configurations

### Parallel Flow
- Hot and cold fluids enter from the same side  
- Temperature difference decreases along the length  
- Less efficient heat transfer  

### Counterflow
- Fluids move in opposite directions  
- Maintains a higher temperature gradient  
- Results in **greater heat transfer effectiveness**

## Engineering Analysis

To analyze system performance, we applied:

### Mass Balance
- Ensures conservation of mass across the system  

### Energy Balance
- Accounts for heat transfer between hot and cold streams  
- Relates inlet and outlet temperatures  

### Entropy Balance
- Evaluates system irreversibility  
- Confirms heat transfer efficiency and losses

## Key Findings

- **Counterflow configuration is more efficient** than parallel flow  
- Greater temperature gradient improves heat transfer  
- Experimental results aligned with theoretical expectations  

---

## Proposed Improvement

Based on system behavior :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2} (page 3):

- Insulate tubing and exchanger body using foam or fiberglass  
- Reduce heat loss to surroundings  
- Improve overall system efficiency
