## Overview

Spotted lanternflies (SLFs) are an invasive species that severely damage grapevines by extracting sap, reducing yield, and contaminating crops during harvest. Current mitigation methods are either labor-intensive or harmful to the vine.

Our team designed a **trellis-mounted, non-invasive device** that attracts and removes SLFs directly from grapevines using a combination of sensory lures and mechanical capture.

---

## Problem

- SLFs reduce grape yield and vine health  
- Infestations can cause major economic losses (≈ $1.5M in the Finger Lakes in the first year alone)  
- Existing solutions are inefficient, labor-heavy, or damage the vine  

---

## Solution: “The Lure”

We developed a **lure-and-contain system** that operates in two stages:

### 1. Attraction
- Wintergreen oil (known SLF attractant) draws insects into the device  
- Potential upgrade: 60 Hz acoustic lure  

### 2. Capture
- Internal geometry guides SLFs downward  
- A **motor-driven rotating mechanism** pushes insects into a sealed collection chamber  
- Prevents escape and allows continuous operation  

---

## Prototype Design

- Trellis-mounted system powered by battery  
- Uses a geared DC motor and rotating “toothed” mechanism  
- Includes removable collection chamber for maintenance  
- Designed to operate continuously throughout the growing season  

---

## Testing & Results

We evaluated the mechanical performance of the system:

- **Rotation:** Achieved full 360° motion with no internal interference  
- **Voltage Requirements:**  
  - ~2.47 V to start  
  - ~1.74 V to maintain rotation  
- **Operating Speed:** ~45 rpm minimum  

Results confirm the system is **mechanically viable**, though motor speed optimization is needed.

## Key Benefits

- **Non-damaging to vines** — no chemicals or direct contact  
- **Low labor requirement** after installation  
- **Scalable design** for vineyard-wide deployment  
- **Cost-efficient** relative to existing solutions  

---

## Challenges & Tradeoffs

- Maintenance required to empty collection chambers  
- Large-scale deployment may increase cost  
- Effectiveness depends on SLF attraction behavior  
- Requires further real-world validation  

---

## Future Improvements

- Optimize motor speed for smoother operation  
- Integrate solar power for long-term deployment  
- Validate capture efficiency with live SLFs  
- Improve structural robustness for vineyard conditions

## Takeaways

This project demonstrated how **mechanical design, biology, and real-world constraints** intersect.

We moved from concept → prototype → testing while balancing:
- engineering feasibility  
- cost and scalability  
- client needs  

The result is a **practical, non-invasive solution** with strong potential for real-world impact in agriculture.
